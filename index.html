अहम् ब्रह्मास्मि

Aham Brahmasmi — "I am Brahman." One of the four Mahāvākyas (great sayings) of the Upanishads, from the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad of the Yajur Veda.

अहम्  ·  ब्रह्मास्मि
Aham  ·  Brahm&āsmi

Aham (अहम्) — I, the pure subject of awareness — not the body, not the mind, not the personality.

Brahman (ब्रह्म) — the infinite, the absolute, the ground of all being.

Asmi (अस्मि) — I am. Not "I will become" or "I was" — but the eternal present tense of existence itself.

The teaching is not belief or metaphor — it is direct recognition. The one who hears the mantra and the one who chants it are the same awareness. Repetition is not affirmation but return — a continuous pointing toward what is already true.

How to Sit With This

This is not music to listen to. It is a mirror to look into.

Before pressing play: Sit upright. Close your eyes. Let three natural breaths settle the body. Feel the weight of sitting.

As the chanting begins: Do not follow the words with your thinking mind. Let the sound arrive as sensation — vibration in the chest, in the skull, in the space behind the eyes. The mantra is not addressed to you — it is spoken as you.

During silence: The pauses between repetitions are the teaching. Resist the urge to anticipate the next chant. Rest in what remains when sound stops. That remainder — aware, still, without content — is what the mantra points to.

Minimum session: 15 minutes. The first 5 minutes are adjustment. The real sitting begins around minute 8.

Three Modes

Standard
For open-eye or closed-eye daytime meditation. Natural pace. No binaural.
Headphones ◎
Adds a 4 Hz theta binaural beat (use with headphones). Deepens inner stillness. Slightly slower cycle with longer silence gaps.
Sleep ◑
3.5 Hz delta binaural. Very slow 50-second cycle — mostly silence. Designed to guide awareness inward as the body falls asleep. Use lying down with headphones.

Binaural beats require headphones to work — they have no effect through speakers.

What You Are Hearing

Human Chanting
Real recording of "Aham Brahmasmi" chanted 108 times. Loops seamlessly. Fades in gently on play, fades out on pause.
Tanpura Drone
Four oscillators tuned to D2 · A2 · D3, the classical Indian tonic-fifth drone. Natural beating between string pairs creates a living, breathing foundation.
Himalayan Singing Bowl
Struck at 432 Hz at the start of each visual cycle. Five natural overtones decay for up to 10 seconds — a gentle temporal marker.
Temple Reverb
Schroeder synthetic reverb — four comb filters, two allpass filters. Simulates a stone temple or cave. Controlled by the Space slider.
Binaural Beats (Headphones / Sleep)
Theta (4 Hz) in Headphones mode · Delta (3.5 Hz) in Sleep mode. Requires headphones. Deepens the meditative state neurologically.

Suggested session: 20 min minimum. The first 8 minutes are settling.
